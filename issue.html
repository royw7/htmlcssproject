What Exactly is Hate Crime?

What is a hate crime?

A hate crime is a criminal offense that is motivated based on their race, religion, color, gender, or sexual orientation.

There are two specific ways of committing hate crimes: Person and Property

ex.

A hate crime against a person could be assaulting a black individual because of color.

A hate crime against property could be vandalism, damage to religious property(arson to church, synagogue, mosque)

What can happen when caught committing a hate crime?

For the first offense, hate crime is a Class 3 felony and for every subsequent offense, it becomes a Class 2 felony.

Psychological effects of hate crime:

Victims of Hate Crime are more likely to experience psychological distress than victims of other violent crimes.

They are more likely to experience post-traumatic stress, safety concerns, depression, anxiety and anger than victims of crimes that are not motivated by bias.

Hate crimes send messages to the victim's groups that they are unwelcomed and unsafe.

Witnessing Discrimination also leads to psychological distress and lower self-esteem.

Following data is from the IACP

  • Hate crimes are often especially brutal or injurious.
  • Victim(s) usually feel traumatized and terrified.
  • Families of victims often feel frustrated and powerless.
  • Others in the community who share the victim's characteristics may feel victimized and vulnerable.
  • Hate incidents can escalate and prompt retaliatory action.
  • Hate crimes and hate incidents create communitywide unrest.
